About This Project

Phoenix Rising Community Solar is a collaborative effort to deploy solar energy infrastructure in five underserved neighborhoods across Phoenix. This project directly addresses energy inequity by making clean energy accessible and affordable to low-income households that have historically been excluded from renewable energy benefits.

Through partnerships with community organizations, local government, and private investors, we've installed rooftop and ground-mounted solar arrays that generate electricity for participating households at rates 15-20% below current grid prices.

Project Goals

  • Provide clean, affordable energy to 1,000+ households by 2026
  • Create 50+ local jobs in solar installation and maintenance
  • Reduce community carbon emissions by 5,000 metric tons annually
  • Build energy literacy and climate leadership among participants
  • Demonstrate replicable model for other cities and regions

Project Timeline

2022

Planning & Community Engagement

Conducted 12 community listening sessions, surveyed 500+ households, and secured initial $800K in grant funding.

Early 2023

Installation Phase 1

Completed rooftop installations on 300 homes. Created 25 local solar technician jobs through training partnership.

Mid 2023

Installation Phase 2 & Launch

Deployed ground-mounted arrays in public spaces. First cohort of 500 households began receiving solar benefits.

2024

Expansion & Optimization

Reached 847 households. Launched monthly community education workshops on energy efficiency and climate action.

2025-2026

Scale to 1,000+ Households

Planning phase: Additional neighborhoods, expanded job training, replicability documentation.
