Local Chapters

Green Horizon chapters are community-led hubs for climate action. Members organize projects, share resources, and build neighborhood power together.

What's a Local Chapter?

A chapter is a grassroots group of Green Horizon members in your area who meet regularly to:

  • Plan and launch local climate projects
  • Share knowledge and best practices
  • Connect with other organizers and community members
  • Advocate for local environmental policies
  • Build community resilience and relationships

No prior experience needed. We provide organizing training, resources, and ongoing support.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need climate or organizing experience?

No. We welcome people from all backgrounds. Training, mentorship, and resources are provided. Your passion and willingness to learn matter most.

Can I volunteer remotely?

Yes. We have remote roles in communications, fundraising, and research. Many in-person roles also have flexible scheduling options.

What's the time commitment?

It's flexible. Some roles require 2-3 hours monthly; others are 10+ hours weekly. You choose what works for your life.

How do donations make an impact?

Every dollar funds projects in underserved communities, educational programs, organizer training, and capacity building. See our transparency report for details.

Can my organization partner with Green Horizon?

Absolutely. We collaborate with nonprofits, schools, businesses, and government agencies. Contact us to discuss possibilities.

How do I start or join a local chapter?

Use our chapter finder above to search by location. If no chapter exists, we'll support you in starting one. Either way, we'll connect you with organizers.
